Field Trip: Six Flags Discovery Museum (Vallejo, California)

Organization: GETSET (Girls Exploring Technology, Science, and Engineering Together)

Date: October 30, 2019

I went on this field trip to Six Flags with a school club called GETSET. Its name stands for Girls Exploring Technology, Science, and Engineering Together. This club provides amazing opportunities for women to explore the STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) fields and see where their interests lie. The core idea behind the foundation of this club is to try to raise the historically low number of women working in STEM fields. During this field trip, we had a behind-the-scenes look at some of the exotic animals housed at the zoo. Specifically, we visited the giraffes, sharks, and dolphins, and learned about how the staff takes care of them, including feeding, cleaning, and vet visits. This experience was interesting because I am also looking into veterinary medicine as a career option, and this trip was very informative and provided a deeper look into the career. Guest Speakers (Engineering Academy)

Guest Speaker: Maynard Holliday

Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)

Topic: Chernobyl, Robotics, and Artificial Intelligence

Date: October 15, 2018

One of the guest speakers I had the honor of listening to while in the class POE (Principles of Engineering) was Maynard Holliday. He was at one time the senior technical advisor to the United States of America's undersecretary of defense for acquisition technology and logistics. He worked on the remediation for the Chernobyl nuclear accident, using both his skills in robotics and artificial intelligence. Holliday was particularly interesting to me, since my career interest is in computer science, specifically in artificial intelligence and machine learning. One key takeaway that I got from this presentation was that machine learning utilizes of the following: speech recognition, digital assistance, fingerprint identification, computational photography, facial recognition, and producing text. In summary, this presentation was helpful to me because it was beneficial to see a professional in the industry, and see his projects and his thinking process through them.

Guest Speaker: Jason Yosinski

Course: AP CSA (Advanced Placement Computer Science Applications)

Topic: Artificial Intelligence & Neural Networks

A guest speaker that we had the honor of meeting while in AP CSA was Jason Yosinski. He obtained his PhD from Cornell University, and is a founding member of Uber AI Labs. He talked about his work with neural networks, and the different projects that he has worked on that implemented neural networks or artificial intelligence. A key takeaway from his presentation was the way neural networks work, and how computer scientists like him work to refine them so that they make less mistakes. To me, his most interesting project was a project where he used neural networks to identify images. He would input an image, and the network would tell him what it thought the image was of, and how certain it was that it was that answer. The network identified images by looking for patterns. For example, there was one pattern of pure black, and one of pure white. Then, it looked for edges. For example, black on the left of a straight edge, and white on the right, or other such patterns. Through a trial and error system with many images, the neural network eventually got to the best it would be. The engineers found out that this was the end of refinements they could make to the system because some outliers would throw the system off, but the majority of the images would be recognized correctly.
