Guest Speakers (Engineering Academy)

Guest Speaker: Maynard Holliday

Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)

Topic: Chernobyl, Robotics, and Artificial Intelligence

Date: October 15, 2018

One of the guest speakers I had the honor of listening to while in POE (Principles of Engineering) was Maynard Holliday. He was at one time the senior technical advisor to the United States of America's undersecretary of defense for acquisition technology and logistics. He worked on the remediation for the Chernobyl nuclear accident, using both his skills in robotics and artificial intelligence. Holliday was particularly interesting to me, since my career interest is in computer science, specifically in artificial intelligence and machine learning. One key takeaway that I got from this presentation was that machine learning utilizes of the following: speech recognition, digital assistance, fingerprint identification, computational photography, facial recognition, and producing text. In summary, this presentation was helpful to me because it was beneficial to see a professional in the industry, and see his projects and his thinking process through them.

Guest Speaker: Laura Kegelmeyer

Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)

Topic: NIF (National Ignition Facility at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory) & Machine Learning

Date: January 11, 2019

Another guest speaker in POE (Principles of Engineering) that presented in class was Laura Kegelmeyer who worked at the NIF (National Ignition Facility) at Lawrence Livermore Nation Laboratory. Her main topic was machine learning, similar to Maynard Holliday, but used in a different situation. A key takeaway from her presentation was what NIF does. It studies fusion and ignition, and therefore helps to explore the cosmos, and enables clean energy security for humankind. This presentation inspired me because it showed that there are useful applications for engineering this concept could help humanity, which has driven me to continue studying engineering in general and especially machine learning. Overall, Kegelmeyer's presentation helped me see the amount of good that engineers can do for the world, making technology and equipment work together with people to better the lives of many.
